About QR Codes

QR (Quick Response) codes are two-dimensional barcodes that can be scanned by smartphone cameras and other devices to quickly access information or perform actions.

Common Uses for QR Codes

  • Website URLs: Direct users to your website without typing a URL
  • Contact Information: Share contact details that can be saved directly to a phone
  • WiFi Access: Allow quick connection to WiFi networks without typing passwords
  • Product Information: Link to product details, manuals, or authenticity verification
  • Marketing Materials: Use on business cards, flyers, posters, and packaging
  • Digital Payments: Facilitate easy payment methods

QR Code Types

This tool supports several QR code types:

  • Text/URL: Standard text or website links
  • vCard: Contact information that can be added to address books
  • WiFi: Network connection details for easy WiFi access

Error Correction Levels

QR codes include error correction capabilities that allow them to be readable even if partially damaged or obscured:

  • L (Low): 7% of data can be restored
  • M (Medium): 15% of data can be restored
  • Q (Quartile): 25% of data can be restored
  • H (High): 30% of data can be restored

Higher error correction levels make the QR code more reliable but also more complex (with more modules/pixels).
